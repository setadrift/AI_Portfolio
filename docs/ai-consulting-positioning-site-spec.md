# AI Consulting Positioning and Site Implementation Spec

Date: 2026-06-25

## Goal

Make Duncan's AI consulting offer feel specific, concrete, and easier to buy.

The current site and ad landing pages already say the right broad thing: practical AI workflow automation for small businesses. The issue is that the offer can still read as "bring me any AI problem and we will figure it out together." The site should instead lead with a narrow operating promise:

> I build review-first AI workflows for service businesses buried in emails, PDFs, forms, quotes, photos, spreadsheets, and follow-up.

The site should sell a clear first step, then show a small set of repeatable workflow systems that Duncan can audit and build.

## Research Findings

### 1. Specialists beat generalists in consulting marketing

Consulting Success summarizes the market pressure clearly: generalist marketing is commoditized, while specialists by industry, channel, or model command more value. Their consulting-market breakdown also states that the fastest fee growth comes from narrowing the offer rather than adding more services.

Source: https://www.consultingsuccess.com/types-of-consulting

Implication for the site:

- Do not lead with "AI consulting for small business" as the only message.
- Lead with a narrower market plus a narrow job-to-be-done.
- Use "small service businesses" and "admin-heavy operating workflows" as the umbrella.

### 2. Vertical specialization makes the business more memorable

Duct Tape Marketing's vertical specialization guidance emphasizes "anyone, not everyone": a focused vertical helps agencies position as experts, differentiate, and attract ideal clients.

Source: https://ducttapemarketing.com/vertical-market-specialization-blueprint/

Implication for the site:

- Keep vertical pages, but do not present all verticals equally on the homepage.
- Promote 3-4 strongest vertical workflow categories first.
- Let the rest remain SEO/ad-specific pages until there is proof or volume.

### 3. Productized consulting is easier to buy

Consulting Success defines a productized model as a fixed-scope, fixed-price offer delivered the same way each time. That is easier to sell and creates a repeatable sales/delivery process.

Source: https://www.consultingsuccess.com/how-to-start-a-consulting-business

Implication for the site:

- Package the entry offer as a fixed-scope AI Workflow Audit.
- Make the deliverables explicit.
- Stop asking prospects to imagine the whole engagement from scratch.

### 4. Good AI workflow audits are specific, fast, and deliver an action plan

SignalARC describes an AI workflow audit as a structured review of actual operations that returns a prioritized action plan, not a generic list of AI tools. Their example deliverables include an impact/effort matrix, tool recommendations, ROI projection, quick-start plan, and cost of doing nothing.

Source: https://signalarc.io/blog/ai-workflow-audit

Ajaia describes similar audit contents: workflow mapping, bottleneck review, automation scoring, data/system readiness, risk review, ROI framing, and next steps.

Source: https://ajaia.ai/ai-workflow-audit

Implication for the site:

- The audit page should say exactly what the buyer gets back.
- The form should ask for workflow volume, current tools, and the specific bottleneck.
- The CTA should be "Request a workflow audit" or "Book a 15-minute fit call," not "get in touch."

### 5. The best first workflow is frequent, measurable, and reviewable

Layer3Labs frames the best first AI workflow as frequent, measurable, and easy to review, with clear inputs, repeatable decisions, and obvious next steps.

Source: https://www.layer3labs.io/ai-workflow-audit

Implication for the site:

- Add a "Good first workflow" checklist.
- De-emphasize one-off creative tasks, vague strategy, and risky automations without human review.
- Explain that Duncan starts with one workflow, not a whole-company AI transformation.

### 6. Workflow partner positioning is stronger than one-off AI project positioning

Moxo's workflow-partner model argues that AI automation agencies struggle when they sell one-off custom projects and do better when they own operational outcomes over time. It also emphasizes reusable execution patterns and human-in-the-loop design.

Source: https://www.moxo.com/blog/starting-ai-automation-agency

Implication for the site:

- Present the audit as the first step toward a managed workflow build.
- Use "review-first" and "human approval stays in the loop" as trust language.
- Offer optional implementation/support after the audit.

### 7. Small-business AI consulting should include build-vs-buy judgment

Arsum's small-business AI consulting guide notes that smaller B2B/team engagements should move faster than enterprise AI work and should include workflow audit, prioritization, build-vs-buy decision, implementation, integration, and documentation. It also says a consultant should tell clients what not to automate.

Source: https://arsum.com/blog/posts/ai-consulting-for-small-businesses/

Implication for the site:

- Duncan should position as the operator who decides "automate, redesign, buy, or leave manual."
- Add explicit "What I will not automate" and "What should stay human" language.
- This differentiates from generic chatbot/automation agencies.

### 8. The strongest current examples use admin pain, not AI novelty

Newbloom AI's audit page leads with owner-led businesses buried in repetitive admin across emails, PDFs, photos, forms, spreadsheets, quotes, reports, customer intake, and repeated follow-up.

Source: https://newbloomai.com/ai-workflow-audit

Implication for the site:

- Use admin objects and workflow artifacts in copy: emails, PDFs, photos, quotes, forms, spreadsheets, portals, CRM notes.
- The pain should sound operational and recognizable, not futuristic.

## Recommended Positioning

### Primary positioning

> AI workflow systems for service businesses with too much admin between the customer request and the finished job.

### Stronger homepage headline options

Option A:

> AI workflows for service businesses buried in admin

Option B:

> Turn emails, forms, photos, quotes, and follow-up into one reliable workflow

Option C:

> Practical AI automation for the work your team keeps tracking manually

Recommended: Option B for landing pages, Option A for the homepage.

### One-sentence explanation

> I help owner-led service businesses map one painful workflow, decide what should be automated, and build a review-first system your team can actually use.

### What to stop saying as the lead message

- "AI consulting for small business" without a specific workflow.
- "Let's figure out whatever AI solution you need."
- "AI transformation."
- "Custom AI solutions" without examples.
- "Save time with AI" as the main promise.

These can remain secondary context, but they should not be the first-viewport promise.

## Offer Architecture

### Entry offer: AI Workflow Audit

Purpose: low-friction first engagement that turns vague AI interest into a concrete build plan.

Recommended package:

- Name: AI Workflow Audit
- Buyer: owner-led service business or small team with repetitive admin
- Format: intake form plus 30-45 minute workflow call
- Turnaround: 2-3 business days after call
- Output:
  - Current workflow map
  - Bottleneck and failure-point list
  - Automate / keep human / redesign / buy recommendation
  - First automation build plan
  - Tool/data readiness notes
  - Risk and human-review boundaries
  - Estimated time savings or revenue-leak hypothesis

Pricing recommendation:

- If optimizing for lead volume from ads: keep the first call free, then offer a paid audit.
- If optimizing for seriousness: publish "Fixed-scope audit available after fit call" and test CAD $750-$1,500.
- Suggested starting point: CAD $950 for the audit, credited toward implementation if they proceed within 14 days.

### Implementation offer 1: Revenue Follow-Up Workflow

Best for:

- Roofing contractors
- HVAC/plumbing service companies
- Med spas or other consult-driven local services

Pain:

- Leads, estimates, consults, quotes, repair recommendations, or rebooking opportunities go stale because follow-up is spread across inboxes, texts, CRM notes, and memory.

Build examples:

- Stale quote dashboard
- Next-follow-up owner and date
- AI-assisted follow-up drafts with human approval
- Lost-reason tagging
- Production or service handoff checklist

Primary pages to promote:

- `/en/ai-workflow-audit/roofing-estimate-follow-up`
- `/en/ai-workflow-audit/hvac-plumbing-dispatch-follow-up`
- `/en/ai-workflow-audit/med-spa-lead-intake-follow-up`

### Implementation offer 2: Document Intake and Review Workflow

Best for:

- Commercial subcontractors
- Property managers
- Bookkeepers/accounting teams
- Insurance brokerages
- Legal intake teams

Pain:

- PDFs, forms, email threads, photos, receipts, tenant requests, bid packages, client docs, or renewal information arrive messy and require repeat review.

Build examples:

- Intake queue
- Missing-info checklist
- AI summary and classification
- Review queue with human approval
- Airtable/CRM record creation after approval
- Status and aging dashboard

Primary pages to promote:

- `/en/ai-workflow-audit/construction-bid-package-review`
- `/en/ai-workflow-audit/property-management-maintenance-intake`
- `/en/ai-workflow-audit/bookkeeping-receipt-cleanup`
- `/en/ai-workflow-audit/insurance-renewal-intake`
- `/en/ai-workflow-audit/legal-client-intake`

### Implementation offer 3: Operations Inbox and Admin Queue

Best for:

- Owner-led firms where work arrives through shared inboxes, forms, texts, and spreadsheets
- Businesses with no clean system of record

Pain:

- Staff are manually triaging, copying, chasing, and updating status across tools.

Build examples:

- Shared inbox triage
- AI labeling and missing-info detection
- Priority queue
- Owner/status aging
- Drafted replies for review
- Weekly operations report

Primary use:

- This should be the generic fallback offer when a lead does not fit a vertical page.
- It can replace "general AI consulting" on the homepage.

## Copy Bank

### Short positioning

> Review-first AI workflow automation for service businesses.

### Longer positioning

> I help owner-led service businesses turn messy admin workflows into practical AI-assisted systems, starting with the emails, PDFs, forms, photos, quotes, spreadsheets, and follow-up that already cost time or revenue.

### Good first workflow copy

> The best first AI workflow is frequent, measurable, and easy to review. It has clear inputs, repeatable decisions, and a human approval point before anything important changes.

### Human-in-the-loop copy

> The goal is not to remove judgment. The goal is to prepare the right context, draft the next step, flag the exception, and let the right person approve.

### Bad-fit copy

> This is not a broad AI transformation program, a chatbot demo, or a promise to automate everything. If the workflow is too rare, too risky, or too unclear, the audit will say so.

### CTA variants

- Request a workflow audit
- Book a 15-minute fit call
- See the first workflow to automate
- Find the workflow leak
- Map one admin bottleneck

## Open Decisions

1. Publish audit pricing now or keep pricing behind a fit call?
   - Recommendation: test "Fixed-scope audit available after a fit call" first, then publish CAD $950 if leads are too vague.

2. Choose one flagship vertical or keep three offer paths?
   - Recommendation: keep three offer paths for now because the existing site and ads already support multiple verticals. Put most homepage weight on service businesses and admin-heavy workflows.

3. Should cold outreach point to the general audit page or vertical pages?
   - Recommendation: point each email to the relevant vertical page when possible; otherwise use `/en/ai-workflow-audit`.

## Definition of Done

This positioning update is complete when:

- The homepage no longer leads with generic AI consulting.
- `/en/ai-workflow-audit` presents a fixed-scope entry offer with clear deliverables.
- The site promotes three repeatable workflow systems instead of a loose list of AI services.
- Vertical pages reinforce the same audit package and include first-build examples.
- The form captures enough workflow context to qualify leads.
- Navigation and metadata point buyers toward the workflow audit.
