# AI Consulting Site Design Constitution

Date: 2026-07-18

## Buyer and decision

The primary reviewer is an owner or operations lead at a service business. Within ten seconds they should understand the workflow problem Duncan solves, the operating result, the evidence behind the claim, the safety boundary, and the next action.

## Direction

The site is a founder-led operations studio: quiet, specific, evidence-heavy, and visibly safe. It must not resemble a generic AI agency or SaaS template.

## First-viewport contract

Every primary acquisition page must contain:

1. A recognizable audience or operating situation.
2. A concrete recurring problem.
3. The change Duncan creates.
4. One verifiable proof point.
5. One primary CTA and one secondary proof path.

## Visual system

- Warm off-white canvas, ink/navy text, white evidence surfaces, restrained blue action color.
- Blue indicates action or navigation. Green is reserved for verified positive outcomes, amber for review boundaries, and red for errors or risk.
- DM Serif Display carries editorial headlines. Outfit carries body and interface text. Monospace is reserved for evidence labels, sequence, and operational metadata.
- Use borders, rules, whitespace, and alignment before shadows. Shadows are limited to primary evidence surfaces.
- Corners remain square or modest. Do not introduce decorative pills.
- Cards are allowed only for discrete repeated objects or engagement choices. Never nest cards.
- Real product screens, representative workflow artifacts, and natural founder photography take priority over illustrations.

## Banned patterns

No decorative gradients, glow, glassmorphism, floating shapes, animated particles, generic AI brains, empty “AI-powered” language, equal-weight feature-card walls, invented testimonials, or unverified metrics.

## Content hierarchy

1. Fit, outcome, proof, and next action.
2. Recognizable workflow situations.
3. Ways to work together.
4. Concise evidence.
5. Safe production method and human boundary.
6. Founder identity and accountability.
7. AI/data-use trust statement.
8. Contact and response expectation.

Technical architecture belongs in case studies or progressive disclosure, not the primary acquisition journey.

## Responsive rules

- Mobile presents one decision at a time and retains the complete content story.
- Reflow must work at 320 CSS pixels and 400% zoom without two-dimensional scrolling.
- Navigation, forms, proof, and CTA meaning remain equivalent across breakpoints.
- Viewport review baseline: 390×844, 768×1024, and 1440×1000.

## Motion rules

Motion may reveal hierarchy or explain sequence. It must not create decorative ambient movement. Every animation and smooth scroll must stop under `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`.

## Accessibility gate

WCAG 2.2 AA is required: semantic headings and landmarks, keyboard operation, visible and unobscured focus, persistent form labels, useful errors, descriptive links, alternative text, contrast, non-color meaning, and adequately sized targets.

## Performance gate

Measure field results at the 75th percentile against LCP ≤ 2.5 seconds, INP ≤ 200 milliseconds, and CLS ≤ 0.1. Images need explicit dimensions and responsive sizes. The LCP asset must not be lazy-loaded.

## Evidence rules

Every case study distinguishes live product, client delivery, confidential employment work, representative reconstruction, or prototype. Never invent client usage, revenue, time savings, quotes, or production status. State what Duncan owned and what remained human.

## Conversion event contract

Measure primary CTA clicks, proof-path clicks, booking clicks, workflow-audit submissions, and homepage inquiry submissions. Keep Google Ads conversion tracking intact. Compare a pre-launch baseline against a 14–30 day post-launch window before making causal claims.

## Future-page checklist

Before implementation, define: reviewer, first decision, first-viewport contract, dominant visual form, proof source, trust boundary, mobile form, all UI states, analytics events, and verification viewports.
