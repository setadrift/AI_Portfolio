# AI Automation Client Acquisition Plan - 2026-07-01

## Diagnosis

The cold email batch failed for a structural reason: it targeted likely-fit businesses, not people showing current buying intent. The June 22 list was built from public business directory records. That creates a timing problem: even if the pain is real, the prospect has not recently asked for automation help, posted a job, complained about a broken workflow, or invited outside help.

The Reddit/Alex Parker win worked because it started from visible intent. The next client search should bias heavily toward people already asking for help in public.

## Current Market Read

1. Tool-community job boards are producing the clearest near-term demand.
   - n8n Community has recent freelance AI automation engineer posts for lead generation, agent workflows, APIs, and ongoing workflow maintenance.
   - Make Community has a live Hire Help category with AI automation, Make, Airtable, GHL, Shopify, Klaviyo, Bubble, Supabase, Claude, and Twilio requests.
   - Airtable Community Jobs Board has recent posts for Airtable/Make/API/AI agent intake and internal systems work.

2. General freelance marketplaces still have demand, but they are expensive and noisy.
   - Upwork has large AI job volume, but connects get consumed quickly and proposal competition is high.
   - Use Upwork only for unusually strong matches, not broad bidding.

3. The better wedge is not "AI automation consultant."
   Buyers are asking for concrete outcomes:
   - Fix my Make/n8n workflow.
   - Connect Airtable, forms, payments, and approvals.
   - Build lead intake and follow-up.
   - Add human approval checkpoints.
   - Clean up CRM/reporting operations.
   - Turn messy client or donor data into a usable workflow.

4. Productized trust beats abstract positioning.
   The first paid offer should be small and specific:
   - "48-hour automation rescue"
   - "Airtable/Make intake workflow sprint"
   - "CRM lead follow-up audit and prototype"
   - "Human-review AI workflow build"
   - "Ops dashboard and handoff cleanup"

## Channels To Prioritize

### A. Daily intent-led community replies

Target:
- n8n Community Jobs
- Make Community Hire Help
- Airtable Community Jobs Board
- Zapier Community troubleshooting threads
- Reddit CRM, smallbusiness, AiAutomations, agency, realestateinvesting, bookkeeping, nonprofit, dentistry, roofing, HVAC, property management

Operating rule:
- Reply publicly with useful diagnosis first.
- Do not drop a website link in public unless explicitly invited.
- DM only when they ask for paid help, invite contact, or engage with the public reply.
- Track every reply as a pipeline item.

Daily quota:
- 10 public helpful replies
- 3 direct DMs to explicit paid asks
- 1 deeper mini-audit posted or sent

### B. Agency subcontracting

Target agencies that sell automation but are likely capacity-constrained:
- Make/n8n/Zapier freelancers posting "Offer Help" threads
- small AI automation agencies on LinkedIn and communities
- web design/dev shops selling AI add-ons
- CRM/RevOps freelancers who need implementation support

Pitch:
"I can take scoped automation builds off your plate: Airtable/Make/n8n, API glue, AI review steps, CRM handoffs, and production QA. I am best for the messy implementation work after the sale."

Why this matters:
Agencies already have demand. You do not need to convince the end buyer that automation matters; you need to become overflow capacity.

### C. Trigger-based cold outreach

Do not send another broad 100-email batch.

Only cold email when there is a trigger:
- hiring operations/coordinator/admin roles
- recent job post mentioning Zapier, Airtable, CRM, reporting, AI, Make, n8n
- public complaint about manual process
- visible form/intake/booking bottleneck
- business launching a new location/service
- recent funding or growth signal

Email should mention the trigger in line one and offer one narrow workflow.

### D. Productized public proof

Build one page or one short PDF for each offer:
- CRM lead follow-up workflow
- Airtable/Make intake and approval workflow
- PDF/document extraction workflow
- Ops dashboard and handoff tracker

Each should include:
- problem
- before/after
- screenshots or architecture diagram
- 48-hour paid starter scope
- price anchor or "starting at"
