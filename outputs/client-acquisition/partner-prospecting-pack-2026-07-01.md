# Partner Prospecting Pack - AI Automation Subcontracting - 2026-07-01

Purpose: create a second path to paid work that does not depend on Upwork connects or broad cold email.

The target here is not the end buyer. The target is an automation freelancer, small agency, CRM consultant, or no-code operator who already has demand and may need a reliable builder behind the scenes.

## Why This Channel

- Make Community keeps surfacing active Hire Help threads around Make scenarios, routers, webhooks, APIs, CRMs, databases, AI steps, Google Sheets, and ongoing contractor work.
- n8n Community keeps surfacing freelance/collaboration threads for AI automation, sales automations, workflow tutoring, and remote automation experts.
- Competitor replies are often generic "I can help" pitches. Duncan can stand out by offering implementation capacity, QA, review gates, docs, and reliability instead of broad agency claims.

## Target Profiles

### A. Automation agencies with public hiring or overflow signals

Look for:

- hiring ongoing Make/n8n automation engineers
- mentioning client projects, QA, documentation, or training
- asking for examples/screenshots/portfolio
- recurring "Hire Help" posts with many replies

Message angle:

```text
Hi [name], I saw you are hiring or coordinating Make/n8n automation work.

I am looking for subcontract implementation work, not to compete for your clients. I can take scoped builds or rescue work off your plate: APIs, Make/n8n scenarios, Airtable/CRM handoffs, AI review steps, logging, QA, and handoff notes.

Relevant page: https://www.duncananderson.ca/en/ai-workflow-audit
```

### B. Solo Make/n8n/Airtable sellers with Offer Help posts

Look for:

- sellers with a clear niche but broad project intake
- public portfolios that suggest they sell strategy but may outsource build work
- posts mentioning Make, n8n, Airtable, GHL, CRM, OpenAI, webhooks, APIs

Message angle:

```text
Hi [name], I saw your Make/n8n/Airtable work.

If you ever need another builder for overflow or behind-the-scenes implementation, I can help with brittle scenarios, API glue, Airtable/CRM cleanup, AI review steps, and QA docs. I am specifically looking for subcontract work where you own the client relationship.

Relevant page: https://www.duncananderson.ca/en/ai-workflow-audit
```

### C. CRM / RevOps consultants

Look for:

- consultants posting about HubSpot, Pipedrive, GoHighLevel, Salesforce, Airtable, or client onboarding
- people who sell CRM setup but not custom automation/debugging
- agencies offering sales ops or marketing ops retainers

Message angle:

```text
Hi [name], I noticed you work on CRM/RevOps systems.

If you ever need implementation support on the automation side, I can help connect the messy pieces around forms, lead routing, CRM updates, email/SMS handoffs, AI review steps, and reporting. I am not trying to own the client relationship, just looking for scoped build work when you need extra hands.

Relevant page: https://www.duncananderson.ca/en/ai-workflow-audit
```

## Short Public Reply For Hiring Threads

Use when a forum thread invites replies and a public comment is appropriate.

```text
This is close to the implementation work I do: Make/n8n scenarios, API/webhook glue, Airtable/CRM handoffs, AI review steps, and QA/handoff notes.

I am strongest where the workflow already exists or is already sold, but needs someone to make the build reliable enough for real operations. I can DM examples if that is useful for the role.
```

## Qualification Checklist

Only message partner targets when at least two are true:

- They recently posted or replied.
- They mention client projects, overflow, ongoing work, or hiring.
- They sell Make/n8n/Airtable/CRM automation.
- They need implementation, debugging, QA, docs, or support.
- They have an obvious way to contact them without violating platform norms.

## Daily Partner Block

1. Search Make Community Hire Help and Offer Help for active Make/n8n/Airtable/API sellers.
2. Search n8n Jobs for remote, freelance, collaboration, or agency terms.
3. Send 5 partner notes per day.
4. Track replies separately from direct leads.
5. If a partner replies, pitch one narrow trial: "send me one broken workflow or one scoped build and I will map the repair path before implementation."
